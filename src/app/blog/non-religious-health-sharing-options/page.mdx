<CalloutBox type="crowdhealth">
CrowdHealth: A New Approach to Health Sharing
</CalloutBox>

**How much does CrowdHealth cost compared to health sharing plans?**
CrowdHealth is a healthcare crowdfunding platform that averaged $140/month for individuals under 55 in 2025 and has no per-event coverage caps. Traditional health sharing ministries like Medi-Share, CHM, and Zion HealthShare use pooled monthly contributions and typically cost $115–$405/month, but most cap eligible sharing at $250,000–$1,000,000 per incident.

<CalloutBox type="presidio">
Presidio: Guaranteed Coverage with No Pre-Existing Limitations
</CalloutBox>

**What are the downsides of using CrowdHealth?**
While CrowdHealth offers a unique approach to health sharing, it's important to be aware of some potential drawbacks. These include voluntary contributions, advisory fees (30-43%), being a newer founded platform, and not meeting insurance mandates in some states.

**Do health sharing plans cover pre-existing conditions?**
Most health sharing ministries have guidelines that may limit coverage for pre-existing conditions. It's essential to carefully review the plan details before enrollment.

**Are health sharing plans regulated?**
Health sharing ministries are not regulated like traditional insurance companies. This lack of regulation means that guidelines can change, and there is no guarantee of coverage in some cases.
