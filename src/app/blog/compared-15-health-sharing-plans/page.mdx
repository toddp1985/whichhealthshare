# How much does CrowdHealth cost compared to health sharing plans?
CrowdHealth is a healthcare crowdfunding platform that averaged $140/month for individuals under 55 in 2025, as opposed to traditional health sharing ministries like Medi-Share, CHM, and Zion HealthShare. These use pooled monthly contributions and typically cost $115–$405/month, but most cap eligible sharing at $250,000–$1,000,000 per incident.

<h2 id="how-do-health-sharing-plans-work">How do health sharing plans work?</h2>
Participants contribute a monthly fee to a shared pool. When eligible medical expenses arise, members can submit claims for reimbursement from the pooled funds. Each plan has its own set of rules and guidelines regarding eligibility, coverage, and cost-sharing.

<h2 id="what-are-the-pros-and-cons-of-health-sharing-plans">What are the pros and cons of health sharing plans?</h2>
**Pros:**
- Lower costs compared to traditional insurance
- Promote personal responsibility for healthcare
- Potential for greater transparency in cost and coverage

**Cons:**
- Faith requirements (some plans)
- Coverage caps
- Not regulated like traditional insurance
- Pre-existing limitations
- Guidelines can change over time
