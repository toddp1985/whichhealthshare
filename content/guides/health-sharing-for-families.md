# Health Sharing for Families: Complete Planning Guide 2026

Families using health sharing save $3,000–$8,000 annually compared to ACA marketplace, but must navigate pre-existing exclusions for dependent children, maternity waiting periods, and family-sized deductibles. This guide covers family-specific plan selection, cost calculations for different family structures, and how to protect dependents with chronic conditions.

As of February 2026, 35% of health sharing members are families with dependents, with highest satisfaction in faith-aligned households with stable health histories.

## Pre-Existing Conditions in Dependent Children

**This is the #1 family concern.** If your child has asthma, diabetes, ADHD, or other chronic condition:

### Impact of Pre-Existing Waiting Period

**Scenario: Child with asthma, family enrolls in Medi-Share (12-month wait)**

- Monthly family commitment: $700
- Annual cost (12 months): $8,400
- Annual asthma medication + visits (out-of-pocket): $2,400
- **Total year 1: $10,800** (no health sharing coverage for asthma)

**After 12 months:** Asthma covered at 90%
- Cost year 2: $700/month premiums + $200 asthma copays = $9,000/year
- **Break-even: Year 2.3**

**Compare ACA marketplace:**
- Monthly family: $1,200
- Annual cost: $14,400
- Asthma covered from day 1
- **No waiting period, immediate coverage certainty**

**Verdict: For children with chronic conditions, ACA is cheaper AND guaranteed.**

## How to Protect Dependent Children with Chronic Conditions

**Option 1: Use ACA for kids, health sharing for parents**

- Parents enroll in health sharing (save $3,000–$5,000)
- Children covered by ACA (covers chronic condition immediately)
- **Total family cost: Hybrid approach, moderate savings**

**Option 2: Delay health sharing enrollment**

- Wait until child's condition is stable 12+ months
- Then enroll entire family in health sharing
- **Total family cost: Savings after waiting period over**

**Option 3: Choose shortest-wait health sharing (Zion, 6 months)**

- Zion has 6-month pre-existing wait (vs. 12 months Medi-Share)
- Cost savings partially offset by higher premiums
- **Total family cost: Balanced approach**

**Option 4: Use Medicaid for children (if eligible)**

- Children qualify by household size/income
- Medicaid covers everything at $0 cost
- Parents use health sharing
- **Total family cost: Lowest (if eligible)**

## Maternity Coverage for Families

Most plans have 9–12 month maternity waiting periods. Plan accordingly:

### If Planning Pregnancy

**Enroll 12 months before attempting conception:**
- Enroll January 2026
- Begin attempting pregnancy January 2027
- Deliver December 2027
- Maternity fully covered (past waiting period)

**Cost savings:** $8,000–$15,000 (delivery covered by plan)

### If Already Pregnant

**Use ACA immediately:**
- Pregnancy is qualifying life event
- Can enroll outside open season
- Maternity covered from day 1
- **Cost: $7,000–$12,000 ACA premiums, but delivery covered**

**Don't enroll in health sharing while pregnant.** Pregnancy is pre-existing; 12-month wait means $8,000–$15,000 out-of-pocket delivery.

## Dependent Coverage Considerations

### Age Limits

Most plans cover dependents until:
- Age 18 (if not in college)
- Age 21 (if in college)
- Age 26 (if on parent's plan, like insurance)

**Verify with plan before enrolling.**

### Newborn Coverage

Newborns are covered immediately under family plans (no separate enrollment). Cost included in family rate.

### Emancipated Minors

Young adults aging out of family plan transition to individual membership. Plan with transition timeline.

## Family-Specific Gaps and Workarounds

### Gap: Preventive Care for Kids

Most health sharing doesn't cover routine pediatric preventive care (annual checkups, vaccines, screenings).

**Workaround:**
- Add direct primary care membership ($30–$50/month)
- Use HSA funds for preventive care
- Buy vaccine package separately

---

### Gap: Therapy for Teens

If your teen needs therapy, most health sharing excludes it.

**Workaround:**
- Online therapy platforms ($60–$300/month) cheaper than traditional therapy
- School-based counseling (free)
- Community mental health centers (sliding scale)

---

### Gap: Braces and Dental

Health sharing doesn't cover orthodontics or preventive dental.

**Workaround:**
- Standalone dental plans ($15–$30/month)
- Discount dental plans (not insurance, but negotiate rates down 10–40%)

## Step-by-Step Family Enrollment

1. **Assess dependent health:** Any pre-existing conditions? (If yes, consider ACA)
2. **Plan pregnancies:** Enroll 12 months before attempting conception
3. **Get quotes:** Call Samaritan, Zion, Medi-Share with your family composition
4. **Calculate total cost:** Include annual share, copays, deductible
5. **Compare to ACA:** Get ACA quote for same family
6. **Check Medicaid:** If eligible, Medicaid costs $0
7. **Choose plan:** Based on cost + faith alignment + family priorities
8. **Enroll:** Most plans have 30–60 day waiting period before coverage begins

## Common Family Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Enrolling Pregnant Family Member

**Wrong:** "We're a family, so maternity is covered."

**Reality:** Pregnancy is pre-existing. 12-month wait = $8,000–$15,000 out-of-pocket delivery.

**Right:** Use ACA if pregnant. Enroll health sharing after delivery.

---

### Mistake 2: Not Budgeting for Dependent Chronic Conditions

**Wrong:** "We can manage the 12-month wait."

**Reality:** Child's asthma medication = $2,000–$3,000/year out-of-pocket during wait. Faster to just use ACA.

**Right:** Calculate out-of-pocket medical costs during waiting period. Compare to ACA.

---

### Mistake 3: Assuming Newborns Cost Extra

**Wrong:** "A newborn will cost us another $150/month."

**Reality:** Most plans include newborns in family rate at no additional cost.

**Right:** Verify with plan; don't budget extra for newborn.

## FAQ

**Can I add a new baby without restarting waiting period?**

Yes. Newborns are covered under family plan immediately. No separate waiting period for newborn.

**What if my child needs emergency care before waiting period ends?**

Plan covers emergency care. Pre-existing exclusions apply to ongoing care, not emergencies.

**Can my college-age child stay on family plan?**

Most plans: Until age 21–26. Verify with your plan. After that, child moves to individual plan.

**What about custody/co-parenting situations?**

Only one parent can cover child on plan. Discuss coverage with co-parent and plan.
